# Jaw cysts and odontogenic keratocysts

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Enucleation of jaw cysts with primary closure; odontogenic keratocysts (incl. syndromic, e.g. Gorlin's) need longer surveillance.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Not a PIFU pathway — radiographic surveillance where indicated.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Large cysts (≥2 cm): face-to-face appointment at 6–12 months for a radiograph to check bone healing/tooth eruption.
- Odontogenic keratocysts: as for large cysts, plus annual radiographic review for 5 years to check for recurrence.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Small cysts (≤2 cm diameter): straight to discharge, no follow-up — letter/telephone call with pathology results.

## Surveillance / review interval

Large cysts 6–12 months; keratocysts annually to 5 years.
