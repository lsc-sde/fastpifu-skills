# Urological cancer follow-up and remote monitoring (bladder, prostate, kidney)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Risk-stratified cancer surveillance suited to CNS-led remote monitoring (app/letter/telephone by patient choice).
Surveillance is independent of routine clinic attendance.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Remote monitoring (app/letter/telephone) by an appropriate clinician who reviews the result and contacts the patient — the standard model for stable cancer surveillance.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Bladder (cystectomy, by risk): bloods/CTU+thorax/USS/GFR at 3, 6, 12 months and yearly to 6 years (high-risk CTU at 3/6/12 months); then 2-yearly surveillance CT-TAP per shared decision.
- Prostate on active surveillance: PSA 3-monthly, MRI at 1 year.
  Post-radical treatment: PSA + function impact at 6–12 weeks then 6-monthly with a treatment-team re-referral threshold.
- Kidney (by risk): CT chest/abdomen/pelvis at intervals (low-risk 1/3/5 years; intermediate 6/12 months then yearly to 5 years; high-risk 3/6/12 months, 18 months, yearly to 5 years then 2-yearly).
- Bosniak 2F cysts: CT with contrast at 6, 18, 30, 48 months.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Bladder: at 6 years move to 2-yearly GP-arranged review (or discharge per shared decision).
- Prostate: discharge at 5 years' follow-up.
- Kidney low-risk: discharge at 5 years; intermediate/high-risk: shared decision on ongoing surveillance after 5 years per fitness/wishes.
- Bosniak 2F: discharge if no change at 48 months.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Cancer type & risk stratification | Sets imaging schedule |
| Time since treatment | Determines surveillance vs discharge |
| Patient fitness/wishes | Shared decision after 5 years |

## Surveillance / review interval

Per cancer-type schedule above; remote monitoring by CNS/appropriate clinician.
