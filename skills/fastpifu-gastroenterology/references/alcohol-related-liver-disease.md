# Alcohol-related liver disease (ArLD)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

ArLD without decompensation; disposition driven by fibrosis stage and risk factors. Signpost to alcohol services.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Non-cirrhotic needing planned-interval repeat fibrosis assessment with a safe/robust PIFU/remote-monitoring system — suitable for PIFU or remote monitoring.
- Cirrhotic — followed per stable-cirrhosis pathway (which itself supports PIFU where safe).

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- 6-monthly follow-up for patients diagnosed at, or on subsequent, fibrosis re-assessment with cirrhosis.
- Intermediate fibrosis — repeat assessment in 1–2 years (could be primary care).
- No safe/robust monitoring system.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Planned repeat fibrosis assessment can be done in primary care — discharge back with advice; no/minimal fibrosis re-assess in ~3 years.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Fibrosis stage | Cirrhotic vs not |
| Ongoing alcohol use / risk | Re-assessment interval |

## Surveillance / review interval

6-monthly if cirrhotic; repeat fibrosis 1–2 years (intermediate) / ~3 years (low).
