# Hepatitis B

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Chronic HBV referred with markers of current infection. Much is automatable / remote.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Virally suppressed, stable, adherent, no renal risk factors — remote/PIFU monitoring, 6–12 monthly (12-monthly for suitable stable patients). Suitable for automation.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Meets treatment criteria / starting treatment — monitor first 3–6 months to confirm adherence and viral suppression.
- Not yet on treatment — HBV DNA and ALT every 6–12 months until criteria met.
- Cirrhosis or non-cirrhotic at-risk-of-HCC — 6-monthly liver USS + AFP; cessation of antiviral therapy must be managed by secondary care with close relapse monitoring.
- Planned cancer treatment/immunosuppression with positive HBV markers — secondary-care treatment plan.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Generally not discharged while monitoring/surveillance indicated; A&G manages those not needing secondary care.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Treatment criteria status | On/eligible vs monitor |
| Viral suppression & adherence | Determines monitoring frequency |
| Cirrhosis / HCC-risk status | Drives 6-monthly USS+AFP |

## Surveillance / review interval

HBsAg ±quantification 12-monthly; DNA/ALT 6–12 monthly; HCC USS+AFP 6-monthly if indicated.
