# Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Suspected/confirmed IBD; stable patients suit remote monitoring and PIFU.
Surveillance independent of clinic.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Stable IBD after initial/subsequent follow-up — PIFU pathway (may be lifelong), with a 12-monthly virtual review and clear written flare information.
- Medicines and faecal-calprotectin monitoring and cancer surveillance deliverable remotely/asynchronously.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Newly confirmed/active disease — face-to-face assessment, treatment and medicines review.
- Surveillance/monitoring due (medicines monitoring, faecal calprotectin, cancer surveillance per BSG).
- Nutrition, bone-health, mental-health or pre-conception needs requiring review.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- IBD excluded and symptoms resolved — discharge to primary care.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Disease activity/stability | Active vs stable |
| Surveillance schedule (BSG) | Cancer surveillance timing |
| Flare-plan & contact info given | PIFU pre-condition |

## Surveillance / review interval

12-monthly virtual review on PIFU; surveillance per BSG, independent of clinic.
