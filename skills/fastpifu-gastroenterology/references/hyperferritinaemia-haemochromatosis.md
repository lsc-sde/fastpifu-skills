# Hyperferritinaemia / haemochromatosis (HH)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

High ferritin and HFE-related haemochromatosis.
Much can be triaged via A&G; confirmed C282Y HH needs secondary-care follow-up.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Non-cirrhotic C282Y HH on maintenance — life-long follow-up delivered remotely/PIFU where a safe/secure system exists; bloods ≥6-monthly in maintenance, venesection when bloods mandate.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Induction-phase venesection (weekly until ferritin <50).
- Raised fibrosis markers or other positive findings needing further assessment.
- Ongoing risk factors (MASLD/ARLD) needing repeat fibrosis assessment.
- Cirrhotic on assessment — enter the stable-cirrhosis pathway.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Hyperferritinaemia without HH/significant iron overload — manage in primary care via A&G (do not bring into secondary-care follow-up).

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| HFE genotype (C282Y) | Confirms HH |
| TF sats & ferritin | Stage and treatment phase |
| Fibrosis assessment | Cirrhosis triggers surveillance |

## Surveillance / review interval

Maintenance bloods ≥6-monthly; venesection as bloods mandate.
Life-long for non-cirrhotic C282Y HH.
