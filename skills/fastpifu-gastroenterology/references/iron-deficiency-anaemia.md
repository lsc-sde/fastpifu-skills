# Iron deficiency anaemia (IDA)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Suspected/confirmed IDA, largely a direct-to-test then primary-care-monitored pathway.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Not a secondary-care PIFU pathway — monitoring is in primary care after investigation.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Diagnostic uncertainty after investigation — further tests (CT colonography, capsule endoscopy, renal-tract USS) before discharge.
- Meets Faster Diagnostic Standard — urgent suspected-cancer pathway.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Non-significant findings after DTT/investigation — discharge to primary care with a management plan, safety netting and FBC monitoring at 3/6/12/24 months per NICE.
- Not IDA, or previously investigated IDA — discharge via A&G.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Confirmed iron deficiency (iron studies) | Confirms diagnosis |
| DTT outcome/histology | Significant vs non-significant |
| FDS criteria | Cancer pathway trigger |

## Surveillance / review interval

Primary-care FBC at 3, 6, 12, 24 months (NICE).
