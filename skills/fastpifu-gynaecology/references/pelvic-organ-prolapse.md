# Pelvic organ prolapse (POP)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Symptomatic POP managed conservatively, with pessaries, or surgically.
Asymptomatic incidental prolapse does not require referral.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Patient close to the BMI cut-off listed for surgery, to allow time for weight loss.
- Conservatively managed patient who may want further assessment/treatment.
- Post-operative patient, to assess for early recurrence — with clear contact information and direct access back to the surgical team.
  PIFU limited to 6–12 months.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Nurse-led TWOC and post-operative care.
- Immediate post-operative complications — seen by urogynaecologist or specialist nurse with direct link to the surgical team.
- Complex non-surgical or complex surgical cases discussed at the urogynaecology MDT.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Asymptomatic incidental prolapse (inside vagina / reaching introitus) — no referral; lifestyle advice in primary care.
- Symptoms resolved after conservative management with no ongoing specialist need.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| POP-Q or objective assessment | Severity & management |
| BMI | Surgical eligibility |
| Management option chosen | Conservative/pessary/surgery |

## Surveillance / review interval

PIFU limited to 6–12 months.
