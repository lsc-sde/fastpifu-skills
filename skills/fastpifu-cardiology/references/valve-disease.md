## Scope

Covers mild-to-moderate native valve disease and stable post-intervention valves: aortic stenosis (AS), aortic regurgitation (AR), mitral regurgitation (MR), mitral stenosis (MS), mitral valve prolapse (MVP), bicuspid aortic valve (BAV), and prosthetic/repaired valves (surgical, TAVI, TEER).
Valve PIFU runs alongside a surveillance-imaging pathway: the patient self-initiates contact for symptoms, while echo at set intervals catches asymptomatic progression.
Severe or symptomatic disease is out of scope for PIFU.

## Universal valve exclusions (RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP regardless of valve)

Any one of these excludes PIFU:

- Severe valve disease of any type.
- Moderate valve disease WITH symptoms (dyspnoea, syncope, chest pain).
- LV dysfunction (EF < 50% for AR/MR; EF < 50% post-intervention).
- Awaiting intervention (surgical or TAVI), or outstanding decisions about care.
- Rapid echocardiographic progression on serial imaging.
- PA systolic pressure > 50 mmHg (or high likelihood of pulmonary hypertension).
- Patient cannot have aortopathy assessed by echo alone, where relevant (BAV/AR) — not suitable for PIFU.

## Subtype rules and surveillance intervals

### Aortic stenosis (AS)
- PIFU when: low-moderate AS (Vmax < 3.5 m/s) with preserved LVEF and normal flow, asymptomatic, stable on serial echo.
- RETAIN when: severe AS (Vmax ≥ 4.0 m/s, mean gradient ≥ 40 mmHg, AVA < 1.0 cm²); symptomatic; rapid progression; low-flow state; awaiting TAVI/SAVR.
- Imaging interval: 1–2 years (echo).
  A conservative "low-moderate" threshold is advised for entry, given the natural history of AS.
  Low-flow conditions are excluded from PIFU.
- Leave PIFU if: new/worsening symptoms; Vmax > 3.5 m/s or AVA < 1.2 cm²; new LV/RV dysfunction; new dysrhythmia; PASP > 50 mmHg.

### Aortic regurgitation (AR)
- PIFU when: mild-to-moderate AR with preserved LVEF, asymptomatic.
- RETAIN when: severe AR; LV dilatation; EF < 50%; symptomatic; awaiting surgery.
- Imaging interval: 1–2 years (echo).
  Consider baseline cross-sectional imaging for aortopathy; if echo cannot assess the aorta, patient is not PIFU-suitable.
- Leave PIFU if: new/worsening symptoms; LV dilatation; new LV/RV dysfunction; progression to severe AR; aortic dimension ≥ 40 mm; PASP > 50 mmHg.

### Mitral regurgitation (MR)
- PIFU when: mild-to-moderate MR, preserved LVEF (≥ 60% for primary MR), asymptomatic, LVESD < 40 mm, PASP < 50 mmHg.
- RETAIN when: severe MR; EF < 60% with primary MR; LVESD ≥ 40 mm; EROA ≥ 0.40 cm²; symptomatic; surgical referral made.
- Imaging interval: 18 months – 2 years (echo) for moderate MR with preserved LVEF and PASP < 50 mmHg.
- Leave PIFU if: new/worsening symptoms; new dysrhythmia; LVEF < 60% or LV dilatation; progression to severe MR; PASP > 50 mmHg; new ≥ moderate TR.

### Mitral stenosis (MS)
- PIFU when: moderate MS (MVA > 1.5 cm²), no haemodynamic compromise, asymptomatic.
- RETAIN when: MVA < 1.5 cm²; symptomatic; awaiting intervention; new RV dysfunction.
  (Note: some patients with MVA < 1.5 cm² are symptomatic and need closer review — not PIFU.)
- Imaging interval: 2–3 years (echo).
- Leave PIFU if: new/worsening symptoms; new dysrhythmia; PASP > 50 mmHg; new ≥ moderate TR; new RV dysfunction; MVA < 1.5 cm².

### Mitral valve prolapse (MVP)
- PIFU when: non-severe MVP (mild-moderate MR if present), asymptomatic, preserved LV, no malignant arrhythmia pattern.
- RETAIN when: severe MR from MVP; MVP with complex/malignant arrhythmia syndrome.
- Imaging interval: 3–5 years (echo) for MVP with no/mild regurgitation.
- Leave PIFU if: new/worsening symptoms; new dysrhythmia; LV dilatation; increase of MR to moderate (may transfer to the moderate-MR pathway); PASP > 50 mmHg.

### Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV)
- PIFU when: mild-moderate non-severe valve disease (AS or AR), asymptomatic, preserved LV, stable on serial imaging, no aortic root dilatation requiring surveillance.
- RETAIN when: severe valve disease; significant aortic root dilatation (> 45 mm) requiring timed surveillance; awaiting intervention; echo cannot assess aortopathy.
- Imaging interval: 2–5 years (echo); 12 months if aortic dilatation > 40 mm.
  Consider baseline cross-sectional imaging for aortopathy.
- Leave PIFU if: new/worsening symptoms; new dysrhythmia; new LV/RV dysfunction; significant aortic dilatation; progression to moderate disease; PASP > 50 mmHg.

### Post-valve intervention (surgical repair/replacement, TAVI, TEER)
- PIFU when: fully recovered, normally functioning prosthesis, stable anticoagulation (therapeutic stable INR for mechanical; DOAC if bioprosthetic), preserved LV, no complications, and a baseline post-op echo exists for comparison.
- DISCHARGE when: fully recovered with no ongoing specialist need documented.
- RETAIN when: EF < 50%; NYHA III–IV; unstable/subtherapeutic anticoagulation (especially mechanical valve + variable INR); prosthetic concern (stenosis, regurgitation, thrombosis); intervention < 3 months; abnormal baseline echo (significant leak, patient–prosthesis mismatch).
- Imaging interval: 1–2 years (echo) for normally functioning TAVI/bioprosthetic/ repair/TEER.
  Mechanical prosthesis with no aortopathy: no routine imaging required (consider TTE at 5 years for isolated mechanical MVR to assess RV/TR).
  Ensure a routine post-op TTE is done before entering PIFU.
- Leave PIFU if: new/worsening symptoms; new dysrhythmia; new LV/RV dysfunction or dilatation; mean valve gradient rise ≥ 10 mmHg vs previous echo; new ≥ moderate TR or new prosthetic regurgitation; PASP > 50 mmHg.

## Borderline / uncertain cases (resolve to RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP, lower confidence)

- Vmax 3.5–4.0 m/s (moderate-severe AS borderline).
- EROA 0.35–0.40 cm² or LVESD 38–40 mm (borderline MR severity).
- EF 45–50% without a clear stability trajectory.
- BAV at the upper end of mild-moderate (Vmax 3.5–3.8 m/s).
- Severity described qualitatively without quantitative echo data — flag as a gap.
- Three or more comorbidities with uncertain interaction, or frailty mentioned but not formally assessed.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Quantitative echo (Vmax / mean gradient / EROA / LVESD / MVA) | Severity drives the verdict; qualitative descriptions are insufficient |
| LVEF (quantified %) | < 50% excludes; 45–50% is borderline |
| Serial echo data | Rate of progression matters as much as current severity |
| Symptom status | A symptomatic moderate lesion excludes PIFU |
| Anticoagulation stability | Unstable INR excludes mechanical prosthesis patients |
| Aortic root dimension (BAV/AR) | > 45 mm requires timed surveillance |

## Surveillance / review interval

As per subtype above.
Patient must be ≥ 18 and understand the PIFU pathway including endocarditis red flags and the need for ongoing imaging.
Imaging requests and result review remain the responsibility of a valve specialist.
