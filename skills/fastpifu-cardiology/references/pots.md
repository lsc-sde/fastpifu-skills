# POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome)

## Scope

Postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome. **There is no dedicated NHS England
PIFU pathway for POTS.** Any assessment must apply the general SOP criteria (see
`non-pathway.md` and the universal SOP section of SKILL.md) and must state in the
output that no condition-specific pathway exists, so the reader knows the verdict
carries more interpretive uncertainty.

## PIFU — suitable (only when general SOP criteria are also met)

- Criterion: Diagnosis confirmed. Supported when: tilt-table or NASA lean test
  documented with HR increment ≥ 30 bpm.
- Criterion: Symptoms improved/stabilised on current management. Supported when:
  the letter describes sustained improvement, not a recent change.
- Criterion: Conservative measures in place (salt/fluid loading, compression,
  graded exercise).
- Criterion: If on medication (ivabradine, propranolol, midodrine), stable dose
  ≥ 3 months with no secondary-care monitoring requirement.
- Criterion: No syncope in the past 3 months (isolated presyncope may be
  acceptable if improving).
- Criterion: Patient actively self-monitoring (symptom diary, HR tracking) and
  returned to meaningful daily activities; understands triggers and re-contact.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed follow-up still needed

- Newly diagnosed (within 3 months) — diagnosis/management not yet established.
- Medication just initiated or being titrated — needs response monitoring.
- Outstanding investigations (autoimmune screen, genetic testing, secondary-cause
  workup).
- Frequent syncope (≥ 1/month) — safety concern.
- Significant functional limitation preventing self-monitoring.
- Comorbidities complicating management (hypermobile EDS, mast cell disorder)
  needing coordinated specialist review.
- No improvement after ≥ 6 months of optimised conservative management — warrants
  reassessment before any PIFU.

## DISCHARGE

Apply general discharge reasoning: if the specialist episode is complete and the
patient is handed to GP with no ongoing specialist role, this is DISCHARGE, not
PIFU. State the wording that supports it.

## Borderline / uncertain (resolve to RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP, lower confidence)

- Medication recently stabilised but < 3 months at current dose.
- Presyncope persisting but described as improving.
- Functional recovery partial / not clearly documented.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Diagnostic test result (tilt/NASA lean, HR increment) | Confirms the diagnosis |
| Syncope frequency | ≥ 1/month is a safety exclusion |
| Medication start date / titration status | Recent changes need monitoring |
| Functional status | Determines self-monitoring capacity |

## Surveillance / review interval

PIFU period 12 months; reassess for continuation or discharge at the end. Provide
written information on triggers and a clear re-contact pathway.

## Source

No dedicated NHS England PIFU pathway. Assessment applies the general NHS PIFU SOP
eligibility criteria. This absence must be disclosed in every POTS output.
