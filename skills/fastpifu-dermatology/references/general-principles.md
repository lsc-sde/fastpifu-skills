## Scope

Cross-cutting GIRFT/BAD 'top tips' for deciding follow-up in any dermatology pathway.
Read alongside the condition-specific files.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Follow-up is for monitoring response to treatment, discharge is not yet possible, and the patient can be educated to use PIFU safely.
- Stable patient on long-term medication who could be reviewed virtually or via PIFU rather than routine F2F.
- Frail patient after skin cancer who can self-examine nodes/scar sites — urgent PSFU/PIFU if concerns (MelFo model).

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Monitoring genuinely requires scheduled specialist review or bloods mandated by the SMPC.
- Unstable disease, or a patient unable to self-monitor or contact the service.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Diagnosed patient with a management plan and back-up flare plan, safely manageable in primary care (ideally after experienced-consultant review).
- Condition excluded by the local threshold policy.
- Benign lesion/BCC excised — write with results, no follow-up.
