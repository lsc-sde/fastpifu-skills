# Inflammatory skin disease (psoriasis, eczema)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Psoriasis/eczema managed topically, on phototherapy, or on systemics/biologics.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Moderate-to-severe disease controlled on maximal topical therapy who may need systemics in future — consider PIFU.
- Post-phototherapy and clear, or stable — consider PIFU.
- Stable on systemics/biologics with limited comorbidities — annual nurse monitoring with telephone consultation may be adequate.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Not clear after phototherapy and may need systemics — follow-up to discuss.
- On systemics with comorbidities — personalise follow-up; bloods only as the SMPC requires (often less than guideline frequency).

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Topically managed and stable — education, written treatment and flare plans, discharge to GP.
