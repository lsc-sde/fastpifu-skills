# Melanoma

<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Cutaneous melanoma, staged follow-up per NICE NG14 and the MelFo reduced-frequency schedule. Early detection of recurrence allows prompt treatment; self-examination supports PSFU/PIFU.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Patient can self-examine and reliably detect lymph nodes/recurrence — a PSFU/PIFU arrangement (as in MelFo) allows safe follow-up with fewer appointments.
- Stage IB–IIC during the lower-frequency tail of the schedule where self-surveillance is appropriate.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Within the scheduled-review window for the stage (e.g. Stage IIA six-monthly for 2 years then annual to year 5; Stage IIB/IIC four-monthly tapering).
- Stage IIIA–IV: personalised follow-up as guided by the SSMDT; on or considered for adjuvant therapy; unresected disease.
- Imaging due per guideline.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Stage 0: single education appointment at one year, then discharge.
- Stage IA: discharge at end of year one.
- Completion of the stage-specific schedule (e.g. Stage IB at year 5; Stage IIIA–IIID/resected IV not on adjuvant at end of schedule).

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Stage (AJCC) | Sets the entire follow-up schedule |
| Whether on adjuvant therapy | Stage III–IV on adjuvant needs SSMDT-led review |
| Self-examination capability | Underpins safe PSFU/PIFU |

## Surveillance / review interval

Stage-dependent per NICE NG14 / MelFo; PIFU/PSFU replaces routine slots where self-surveillance is reliable.
