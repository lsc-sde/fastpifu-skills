# Cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC)

<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

cSCC follow-up by risk category (BAD 2020). High-risk may be safely managed with PSFU.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- High-risk cSCC where the patient can self-monitor — evidence supports safe PSFU (GIRFT further-faster).
- Patient too frail for further treatment — close routine follow-up may be replaced by patient-centred PSFU/PIFU.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- High-risk: four-monthly for 12 months then six-monthly for 12 months (to 2 years).
- Very high-risk: four-monthly for 24 months then six-monthly for 12 months (to 3 years).
- Metastatic: three-monthly for 24 months then six-monthly for 36 months, with longer review per patient factors.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Low-risk cSCC: single post-treatment appointment then discharge.
- Completion of the risk-category schedule (high-risk at 2 years, very-high at 3 years).

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Risk category (low/high/very-high/metastatic) | Sets schedule and PSFU suitability |
| Frailty / fitness for further treatment | May favour patient-centred reduced follow-up |
