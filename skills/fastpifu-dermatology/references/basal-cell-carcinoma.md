# Basal cell carcinoma (BCC)

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

BCC follow-up (BAD 2021).
Most single BCCs are discharged post-operatively.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- History of multiple BCCs with reliable self-monitoring — PIFU/PSFU with access if new lesions appear.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Multiple BCCs likely to develop further tumours/recurrence within 12 months — offer at least yearly follow-up.
- Excised high-risk BCC with close histological margin (<1 mm) — refer for MDT discussion.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Single BCC excised — discharge postoperatively with a standard results/sun-protection letter and stitch-removal advice.
- Frail patient with limited life expectancy and slow-growing BCC where conservative management is chosen.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Single vs multiple BCC | Determines follow-up vs discharge |
| Histological margin | <1 mm high-risk needs MDT |
