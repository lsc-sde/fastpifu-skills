# Carpal tunnel syndrome

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

Day-case carpal tunnel decompression after community management/single steroid injection.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- PIFU 3 months only if the patient is not progressing as expected.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- Hand therapy and face-to-face appointment if ongoing problems (CRPS, nerve damage, scar issues), or after neurolysis/flexor tenosynovectomy.
- Not progressing as expected.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Progressing as expected after 10–14 day suture removal (at GP/local) — discharge.

## Surveillance / review interval

Suture removal 10–14 days; PIFU 3 months (conditional).
