# Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS)

<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

AIS pathway (British Scoliosis Society).
PIFU used whenever clinically appropriate, not as an alternative to discharge.
Paediatric/adolescent pathway.

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- 1 year of PIFU with access into the service after the 1-year follow-up.
- Non-operative: nurse-led virtual monitoring pathway; CNS-led remote appointment and X-ray update.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- 8-week face-to-face follow-up + safety netting; 1-year face-to-face follow-up with same-day X-ray; 2-year X-ray (no appointment) with results letter.
- Planned monitoring or bracing pathway; MDT review with action plans.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Close PIFU and discharge after the 2-year X-ray if stable.

## Surveillance / review interval

8-week and 1-year F2F; 2-year X-ray; 1-year PIFU.
