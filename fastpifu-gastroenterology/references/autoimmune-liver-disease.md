# Auto-immune liver disease

<!-- concept_ids: (completed by data team) -->
<!-- author: derived from GIRFT OP guide module 2 | reviewed_by: <lead> | last_updated: 2026-06 -->

## Scope

AILD referred with abnormal LFTs and/or positive auto-antibodies; treatment and monitoring to complete biochemical response (CBR).

## PIFU — suitable for patient-initiated follow-up

- Achieved CBR with a safe system for long-term management — enrol into PIFU/remote monitoring (potentially life-long), including planned consideration of immunosuppression withdrawal after a suitable interval and criteria.
- Stable once CBR reached — clinic only every 6–12 months.

## RETAIN_FOLLOW_UP — timed specialist follow-up still needed

- First 12 months: bloods every 3 months to confirm CBR, then every 3–6 months.
- Active titration or not yet at CBR.
- Cirrhosis on assessment — stable-cirrhosis surveillance pathway.

## DISCHARGE — no ongoing specialist follow-up needed

- Not typically discharged while immunosuppression/monitoring continues.

## Data that must be present to assess

| Required data point | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| CBR status | Gateway to PIFU |
| Bone-health & fibrosis assessment | Mandated monitoring |
| Cirrhosis status | Triggers surveillance |

## Surveillance / review interval

3-monthly bloods in year 1, then 3–6 monthly; clinic 6–12 monthly once stable.
